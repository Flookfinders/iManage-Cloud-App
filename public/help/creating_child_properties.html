Adding Children

 Top  Previous  Next

When creating child properties in iManage Cloud you can choose to create them one at a time or as a range of children, if they are all on the same parent property. First find the Property that you wish to use to create your children on using the Search box. Once you have found the property and it is displayed in the Explorer either hover over it or select it and click the More actions or More respectively.

From this list you can select to either:

Add Child

If you select Add child you will see a selection of Templates which have been previously created by an Administrator.

•Pick the template that most resembles the type of child you wish to create. If you wish to discover more information on any of the entries in the template then please see the relevant topics under the Property Form.

•First you will be asked to complete the Address details where the SAO (either SAO no or description) is mandatory.

•Notes:

oIf you are a Welsh authority then you may click the Welsh tab and amend any of the details.

oIf you are a Scottish authority and wish to add a Gaelic address then you need to first check the Create Gaelic record box. This will display a Gaelic tab where you may enter the Gaelic details.

•When you are happy click Next.

•Next the Property details are displayed. To change anything click on the BLPU, LPI or Other boxes, make your selections and click Done when finished or Cancel to return to the Property details without changing anything. When you are happy with the Property details click Next. If any of the information is invalid you will see an exclamation mark. Click in the box and you will see more information on what needs correcting.

•Now you may add any Cross references for the child. Click on the  to display the Add cross reference dialog. Pick the Source and enter the Cross Reference and click Done when finished or Cancel to return to the Cross references without changing anything. Note: If the template you have selected automatically adds a Cross Reference then you will just need to click on an entry and enter the Cross Reference as the Source will have been already selected. If you do not want to add this particular Cross Reference then hover over the entry and click the Delete icon. Click Next to place your child on the map.
Note: If you don't want to add any Cross references you can click Skip to proceed directly to the Map placement.

•A map is displayed. The property centroid will already be positioned where the parent property is located. Click on the map of you wish to reposition the child. You can drag the map if you need to move it or use the + - buttons at the bottom left to Zoom in or out respectively. You may also use the scroll bar on your mouse to Zoom in and out. When you are happy that you have positioned your child click Finish to create the property.

•You will see the following dialog:

•You can choose to View the property, add a child or a range of children or just Close the dialog.

Note: If at any point during the process you wish to go back and change anything click the Back button at the bottom left of the screen. You may also cancel the entire process by clicking the X at the top left of the screen.
Add Children

If you select Add children you will also see a selection of Templates which have been previously created by an Administrator. This will allow you to create a range of children on the same parent property.

•Pick the template that most resembles the type of children you wish to create. If you wish to discover more information on any of the entries in the template then please see the relevant topics under the Property Form.

•Next you need to decide if you wish to create your children using the SAO numbering / suffix or the SAO text by clicking one of the buttons. The only difference between these two options is the Range information that appears on the Address details. Enter the Address details and decide what type of numbering you want for your properties i.e. Odds and evens, Odds only or Evens only. Clicking these buttons will change the list of properties that will be created. You may also untick the Include box in the list if you want to exclude a particular property say number 13.

Notes:

oIf you are a Welsh authority then you may click the Welsh tab and amend any of the details.

oIf you are a Scottish authority and wish to add a Gaelic address then you need to first check the Create Gaelic records box. This will display a Gaelic tab where you may enter the Gaelic details.

•When you are happy click Next.

•Next the Property details are displayed. To change anything click on the BLPU, LPI or Other boxes, make your selections and click Done when finished or Cancel to return to the Property details without changing anything. If you wish to discover more information on any of these entries then please see the relevant topics under the Property Form. When you are happy with the Property details click Next. If any of the information is invalid you will see an exclamation mark. Click in the box and you will see more information on what needs correcting.

•Now you may add any Cross references for the properties. Click on the  to display the Add cross reference dialog. Pick the Source and enter the Cross Reference and click Done when finished or Cancel to return to the Cross references without changing anything. Note: If the template you have selected automatically adds a Cross Reference then you will just need to click on an entry and enter the Cross Reference as the Source will have been already selected. If you do not want to add this particular Cross Reference then hover over the entry and click the Delete icon. The same Cross Reference will be added to all the properties. Click Next to place your child on the map.
Note: If you don't want to add any Cross references you can click Skip to proceed directly to the Map placement.
