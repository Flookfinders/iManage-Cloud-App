What is an OWPA?

 Top  Previous  Next

An Object Without Postal Address  [OWPA] is exactly what it says on the tin, an object without a postal address. Usually, when people consider addresses they think purely of residential properties or properties which receive mail. However, in addition to comprehensive sets of these records, the NLPG scope extends far beyond this and includes many OWPAs.

These include for example:

•street BLPU records (used to record where incidents such as road traffic accidents may occur)

•Agricultural

•industrial

•transport and utility infrastructure

•land

•amenities such as car parks and public conveniences

•objects of interest such as monuments and places of worship.

Each LPI entry has a flag to indicate whether or not it is postally-addressable. For each postally-addressable object the relevant information to generate both geographic and postal addresses will be populated. For non postally-adddressable objects, the geographic address information will be provided.
